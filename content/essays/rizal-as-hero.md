José Rizal's heroism was rooted in his unwavering commitment to truth, education, and reform. Through his writings, most notably Noli Me Tángere and El Filibusterismo, he exposed the injustices of colonial rule and inspired a movement for change. His advocacy for peaceful reform and intellectual empowerment set him apart as a visionary leader. Yet, his martyrdom on December 30, 1896, when he faced execution at Bagumbayan, sealed his place in history. Rizal's death was not in vain; it ignited the flames of revolution and united Filipinos in the struggle for independence. His life reminds us that true heroism lies in selfless service and courage in the face of oppression.

## Museo ni José Rizal Calamba

Rizal's story begins in Calamba, Laguna, where he was born in a traditional bahay-na-bato near the town plaza. Though the original house was lost in a land dispute in 1890, the present structure, reconstructed in 1950 by National Artist Juan Nakpil, stands as a tribute to his roots. Inside, visitors find facsimiles of manuscripts, drawings, and household artifacts that reflect the family's lifestyle and deep familial bonds.

## Museo ni José Rizal Dapitan

From 1892 to 1896, Rizal lived in exile in Dapitan, Zamboanga del Norte. Far from Manila, he transformed adversity into opportunity by building water systems, improving the town plaza, and teaching locals. His estate became a hub of learning and livelihood, complete with a school, clinic, and farm. Today, the shrine preserves original objects and replicas of his huts, offering a glimpse into his industrious and compassionate nature.

## Museo ni José Rizal Fort Santiago

Rizal's final days were spent in a cell at Fort Santiago, Manila, before his execution. The shrine now houses artworks, personal belongings, and the most poignant artifact: a fragment of his spine struck by the fatal bullet, encased in an ornate urn. This site stands as a solemn reminder of his ultimate sacrifice for freedom.

## Rizaliana Collection at the National Library

Beyond these shrines, the National Library of the Philippines safeguards Rizal's intellectual legacy through its Rizaliana Collection. Among its treasures are the original manuscripts of Noli Me Tangere, El Filibusterismo, and his farewell poem Mi Último Adiós, all of which have been declared National Cultural Treasures. José Rizal's life and martyrdom continue to inspire generations. Through these shrines and collections, the NHCP and partner institutions ensure that his ideals of patriotism, education, and moral courage remain alive in the hearts of Filipinos.
