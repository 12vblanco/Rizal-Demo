The Hero, Artist, and Ethnographer: José Rizal in the Philippines and in Germany is a digital exhibition that seeks to expand and deepen our understanding of Dr. José Rizal by situating him within the social and scientific currents of his time, currents that shaped his evolving visions of reform and revolution in the Philippines. The exhibition is organized around three major themes: (1) Rizal as a Hero, (2) Rizal as an Artist, and (3) Rizal as an Ethnographer.

Central to the exhibition is the theme of Rizal as an Ethnographer, featuring the 21 Philippine objects he donated in 1888 to the Ethnologisches Museum Berlin (formerly the Berlin Museum of Ethnology). The National Museum of the Philippines (NMP) launched this thematic area on December 30, 2025, to commemorate the 129th anniversary of Rizal's martyrdom.

The Rizal as an Artist theme will be accessible on June 19, 2026, in celebration of his 165th birth anniversary. It will present artworks by and about Rizal from the National Fine Arts Collection of the NMP, the National Historical Commission of the Philippines, and select private collections.

The Rizal as a Hero theme will go live on December 30, 2026, marking the 130th anniversary of his death. It will feature personal paraphernalia enshrined at the Museo ni José Rizal in Calamba, Dapitan, and Fort Santiago, together with his original manuscripts preserved at the National Library of the Philippines.

Beyond these three major themes, the exhibition also includes Rizal as a Scholar, which profiles the individuals who shaped his intellectual development and later became his mentors, compatriots, and brothers in the Propaganda Movement.

Another thematic focus, Rizal in Germany, highlights his time in Berlin and his achievements from February 1886 to May 1887, a pivotal period in his life.

Taken together, the exhibition illuminates Rizal's lesser-known pursuits and passions (his artistic talent, literary imagination, and ethnographic sensibilities) and aspires to keep his legacy alive in the hearts and minds of Filipinos for generations to come.
